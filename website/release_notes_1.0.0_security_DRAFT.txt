Security and testing
--------------------

  Ahead of the 1.0.0 release, PhoenixDKIM was put through a round of security
  and robustness testing beyond the standard conformance suite. The testing
  process is described on the project website's Security page.

  - Coverage-guided fuzzing: new libFuzzer targets exercise the two
    untrusted-input tag-list parsers — the DKIM-Signature header
    (DKIM_SETTYPE_SIGNATURE) and the public-key TXT record
    (DKIM_SETTYPE_KEY) — built on top of AddressSanitizer and
    UndefinedBehaviorSanitizer. Enable with -DPHOENIXDKIM_ENABLE_FUZZERS=ON
    (Clang, requires -DPHOENIXDKIM_ENABLE_ASAN=ON). No crashes, leaks, or
    undefined behaviour were found over tens of millions of executions per
    target.

  - DNS failure-mode matrix: a new test (t-dns-failures) drives a mock
    resolver through every category of lookup failure and asserts the
    transient-versus-permanent classification — a missing selector
    (NXDOMAIN / NODATA) is a permanent "no key", while a timeout,
    SERVFAIL/network error, or truncated reply is a transient failure that
    the MTA retries. This guards against a momentary DNS problem being
    mistaken for a forged or absent signature.

  - Independent-implementation interop cross-check: a revived and modernised
    interop harness (additional-test-suite/dkim-crosscheck.sh) signs a corpus
    of edge-case messages with PhoenixDKIM and verifies each with dkimpy, an
    entirely separate DKIM implementation, hermetically (no live DNS). It is
    wired into CTest (label "interop") and skips gracefully when dkimpy is
    absent.

Testing

  - New CTest targets: t-dns-failures (DNS failure-mode classification) and
